Road Intelligence Model · Calibration

The correction factor: making history match reality

Historical data is incomplete and noisy, so a raw estimate drifts from the truth. A learned compensation / correction factor closes that gap — anchoring the model to the present before we trust it with the future.

① Raw estimate
The deterioration formulas + historical inputs produce a first-pass PCI estimate per segment — directionally right, but biased.
② Correction factor
A learned compensation term absorbs unmodelled effects (data gaps, local materials, micro-climate) and shifts the estimate toward observed condition.
③ Calibrated estimate
The corrected curve tracks reality. Only now is the model trusted to extrapolate forward.

Road Intelligence Model · Ground-truth validation

XenoTrack is the reference value

We break the pilot corridor into segments and measure an actual PCI for each with XenoTrack's 6D LiDAR. The model's calibrated estimate is held against this reference — segment by segment — until predictions and actuals agree.

PREDICTED vs ACTUAL PCI — PER SEGMENTMATCHING
Segmented
The corridor is divided into uniform segments; each carries its own PCI, history & prediction.
Reference
Actuals from XenoTrack are the truth; model output is scored against them (e.g. MAE / R²).
Matched
When predicted ≈ actual across segments, the historical model is validated — and the future becomes predictable.
Note: we surface PCI & distress, not the millions of raw point-cloud returns.
